We use the term information standard to refer to standardised ways of collecting, recording, and exchanging health and social care data.

In this service we refer to 4 types of information standard:

  • technical specifications and APIs specify how information is to be made available technically. This includes how the data is structured and transported, its architecture and associated protocols.

    Browse technical specifications and APIs

  • clinical and care record standards define what information to collect, the purpose of the information and how to format it for consistency of meaning - for example creating a standardised way to register a new patient.

    Browse clinical and care record standards

  • medical and data dictionaries define the format of specific data items in ways that allow them to be consistently represented. Labelling of medicines or formatting of dates of birth are examples. This category also includes reference sets and controlled lists.

    Browse medical and data dictionaries

  • data protection and governance define rules for what information can be legally processed and how that information should be securely handled.

    Browse data protection and governance standards


Not every information standard is listed in this service. You can find out more about which standards are included.
