Benefits of interoperability in health - Standards guidance

Over time, interoperability will make care provision faster and safer. This includes by:

  • giving staff instant access to records so they don’t need to wait for emails or faxes - in an emergency this could save someone’s life
  • reducing the risk of information being shared inappropriately by being able to control digitally who can see it
  • allowing staff to more easily see people’s medical histories so they can give better care
  • reducing the risk of dangerous errors - for example, the mistyped name of a medication could be misinterpreted. Using standards means the name can be checked against a list of approved medications first
  • reducing the burden on people needing to share their medical histories every time they receive care - this can be hard for everyone but especially for people with conditions that affect their memory
  • allowing people to see their own health records giving them more visibility and ownership of their data

The recent coronavirus pandemic - where we had to quickly move patients to wherever there was space to treat them - showed us how important it is to join up IT systems to make care provision easier.
