About this service

The standards directory allows you to find the information standards required for technologies in health and social care to be able to interoperate. Currently these standards are published across a number of government and external websites which can make it difficult to see what exists and is relevant to you.

Find out more about the different types of information standard and interoperability

The standards directory is a signposting service only. It doesn’t include the full documentation for each standard which can be found on the standard owner’s website.

This service is currently in development and is being tested and improved based on feedback. To report a problem or make a suggestion you can email the standards directory team at standards.directory@nhsx.nhs.uk.

Who this service is for

This service is for anyone building or buying digital technologies for the NHS or local authority-funded adult social care settings. That includes IT suppliers as well as healthcare providers and technology buyers ranging from procurement teams to clinical informatics specialists and framework operators.

What standards are covered

The following information standards are listed in the directory:

  • all current approved national information standards published on the NHS Digital website. These have been assured by the Data Alliance Partnership Board (DAPB) and issued with an Information Standard Notice (ISN) which requires all health and adult social care providers to implement the standard
  • API standards and specifications published in the NHS Digital API Catalogue
  • record standards published by the Professional Records Standards Body (PRSB)

In the future more standards are likely to be added to the directory from other sources. Data collections are currently excluded although these may also be added in future.

You can find out more about interoperability standards and interoperability or browse the directory.
