About this service

This service lets users browse and search for different types of information standards relevant for enabling interoperability across health and social care. Standards relating to a particular use case are published in different areas and websites depending on the type of standard and area of expertise of those developing them, so it can be hard for users to find all the information they need to solve a whole problem in an interoperable way.

The Standards Directory aims to make it easier for users to find the standards that are relevant to the service they are building or buying, without having to check all the sites that exist.

This service is currently in development and going through testing and iterations. To report a problem, suggest an enhancement or propose a new feature, you can email the standards directory team at standards.directory@nhsx.nhs.uk

Who this service is for

Anyone building or buying digital technologies for the NHS or local authority-funded adult social care settings who need to find what relevant information standards they should include in the system they are building or buying. That includes IT suppliers such as developers and product managers, as well as care providers and buyers of systems ranging from procurement teams, clinical informatics specialists and framework operators.

Others involved in the development of standards - such as assurance teams, regulators or standard owners and creators - can also benefit from increased accessibility and discoverability of standards.

What standards are covered

This service contains entries for information standards drawn from:

  • all current approved national information standards published on the NHS Digital website, for which the Data Alliance Partnership Board (DAPB) has issued an Information Standard Notice (ISN) to announce new or changes to information standards that fall under section 250 of the Health and Social Care Act 2012
  • API standards and specifications published on the NHS Digital API Catalogue
  • record standards published by the Professional Records Standards Body (PRSB)

It currently excludes data collections, which may be included in the future.

Learn more about the types of standards and what they are for in our guidance on what information standards are.

How this service works

The Standards Directory works as a browsable index, bringing together the different types of standards needed for interoperability so that users can find all the content and standards relevant to their use case and service type.
