How you can improve standards

Standards don’t exist for everything services are trying to do in health and social care. In some cases content standards might exist but corresponding technical standards might not.

What’s important is that where standards and APIs do exist and apply, they are used.

The standards community is for everyone involved in using and developing standards for interoperable health and social care technology.

Find out what people are:

  • working on in the standards community development backlog
  • talking about in the discussions forum

Learn how to:

  • propose a new information standard
  • propose a change or extension to an existing information standard
  • post a new discussion to the forum
  • propose a new information standard
  • report a problem with the standards directory

Community principles

These principles are for the community of people that use and develop information standards for interoperability in health and social care technology.

1. Start with what exists

Reuse as much as possible and iterate based on user needs.

Start by checking what exists in the standards directory.

If something is not in the standards directory, check the standards community development backlog and discussions forum to see whether other community members have made proposals or started discussions on this gap.

Reach out to the community to ask questions, gather examples and learn from others’ experiences.

2. Contribute back and help others

The standards community is for everyone. Anyone can contribute evidence-based proposals and start discussions.

Think beyond your specific use case and aim to develop standards that are scalable, reusable and can evolve over time.

Share research findings and examples with others. Be open to feedback on your work.

Above all, be kind. Encourage others to contribute by being respectful when asking questions or giving feedback.

3. Prioritise openness and honesty

Prioritise sharing proposals in the standards community development backlog and use cases, implementation examples and local specifications in the discussions forum.

Share work in progress as early as possible. Be honest about the amount and nature of your research and findings. Share what works and what does not.

Promote awareness of existing work whenever possible.
