How standards work

Who needs to use data standards

Anyone building or buying digital technologies for the NHS or local authority-funded social care settings need to ensure relevant data standards are used where they exist. These technologies include:

  • electronic health records
  • diagnostic and treatment recommendation tools
  • referral management solutions
  • clinical data collection and research tools
  • patient portals and platforms
  • digital staff rosters and other administrative systems

Data standards may not exist for every circumstance, which is why interoperability relies on communities sharing new standards and APIs to help further standards development.

Correct use of data standards

Follow Information Standard Notices

The government mandates standards adoption in some areas in Information Standard Notices which are issued whenever a new standard needs to be implemented. For example, the NHS Number must be used by all health and social care providers, partner organisations and system suppliers when recording information about a specific person.

Browse services

You’ll need to use NHS Spine you may also want to plug into national services that give access to central datasets as well as use resources such as NHS Email and Wifi.

Information Standards Notices also specify content and technical standards in certain clinical areas, for example pathology. If these apply to you, you must follow them. There are still gaps however and clinical areas with low standards maturity may find the standards they need do not exist or only partially exist.

If Information Standard Notices don’t exist, adopt and feedback on relevant standards for your area, or create new ones.
