Case studies

Creating a read-only shared care record: Bristol

Care settings:

  • GP practices
  • adult social care teams
  • acute hospital trust
  • mental health trust
  • drug and alcohol teams

Population size:

    630,000 people

Region:

    South West

Architecture model:

    Hub and spoke

Products:

  • Orion Health Technology - front-end web portal
  • Medical Interoperability Gateway (MIG) - middleware (GP)
  • Rhapsody Integration Engine - middleware (acute/social care)
  • XDS messaging solution - document exchange

Resources:

Technical specifications:
  • HL7 V.3
  • FHIR Core (planned for future development)
Services and tools:
  • Interoperability toolkit

Download:

  • Download as a PDF

Problem to solve

Support health and social care staff across the region to have access to up-to-date information about the people using their services.

Solution

Chief Information Officers from Bristol City Council and 2 acute trusts set up a pilot project in 2013, funded by the participating care settings. Following a competitive procurement process they selected Orion Health Technology as their solution. The full programme to create a shared care record was rolled out in 2014.

Resourcing

A team of 10 was recruited including:

  • senior solutions architects
  • testers
  • project managers
  • information governance specialists

Between 5 and 10 projects were ongoing at any one time as information for the record was pulled in from more places.

Technology

Users request data through Orion Health Technology’s Connecting Care portal. This either pulls the data directly from the system via the MIG in the case of GPs, or from a central data store using the Rhapsody Integration Engine and Nextgate Master Patient Index in the case of acute/social care settings.

“If you’ve got 15 or 20 systems and you try to join them all up you’ll soon see it’s a massive mess. it’s just not sustainable to do that level of integration.” Project manager

Challenges

Challenges included:

  • getting funding and agreeing how to allocate costs - the team was constantly applying for funding from local and national sources
  • putting in place an effective support strategy that can cover 24 system interfaces, 8000 users and different IT support functions
  • creating a record that worked for social care as the data is very different. The team also surfaced data quality issues that needed fixing.
  • managing ongoing work around security and upgrades as versions go out of date

Results

Healthcare professionals can access care records populated by all care settings including adult and children’s social care, drug and alcohol services and out of hours GP.

Feedback:

“[This has] dramatically decreased the time it takes to undertake a triage.” Care of the Elderly Pharmacist

“It was amazing having this resource over the long weekend. I was able to access information about a patient’s usual insulin regime when she was severely unwell and not able to communicate with us”. Pharmacist, North Bristol Trust

“The great benefit...is access to medical history, contacts with GP and medication lists. Although it is possible to get these faxed from GPs, it is very time consuming and not available 24/7.” Bristol Community Health Service

Aspirations

The team are exploring two-way integrations with patient-facing apps.
