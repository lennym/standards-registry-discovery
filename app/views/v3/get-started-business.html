Common challenges

Common problems people find when attempting to join up IT systems are:

Existing technology doesn’t support data-sharing

Existing technology can mean organisations face challenges accessing and sharing their data. This can happen when information is held within a product or application itself instead of in a separate layer which is owned by the customer.

What this means for individual organisations may vary depending on the supplier, but customers can be charged high fees or required to wait a long time to be able to access their own information. If you’re having problems with your supplier contact the support team at helpme@interop.nhs.uk.

Standards can change

Standardisation is an organic, community-led process - it cannot be dictated by any one organisation. As such because they understand. Some standards in health and social care are well established..This is an area in which resources can go out of date and so they need to be maintained and kept up to date. Teams or contracts need to be set up to be able to deal flexibly with these changes.

Staff don’t use new ways of working

To realise the benefits...This can be harder if systems don’t work in the way staff need them to, they’re confusing and hard to use, or it’s not easy for everyone to access them. Staff may prefer their existing, known ways of working. There’s guidance on how to create clear, easy-to-use digital systems and services that meet people’s needs from the NHS Design System and GOV.UK Design System. ….
